# Echoes of Aethermoor - Gameplay Guide

A complete guide to playing and understanding the mysteries of Aethermoor.

## 🎯 How to Play

### Basic Flow

1. **Read the Game Interface**
   - **Left Panel**: Your current rank, discovered secrets, and rewards
   - **Center Panel**: Chat window showing narrator responses, extraction form for your answers
   - **Right Panel**: Rank progression, hints for current secret

2. **Ask a Question**
   - Type any question about Aethermoor in the "Extract Information" text area
   - Example: "Who created this world?", "What are the four elements?", "Tell me about the collapse"
   - Click "Ask Question" to submit

3. **Read the Response**
   - The narrator responds with a story, metaphor, or philosophical musing
   - **Novice Rank**: Responses are clear and straightforward
   - **Higher Ranks**: Responses become increasingly cryptic and poetic
   - Pay close attention to specific details—they often hide the answer

4. **Extract Information**
   - In the "Extract the information you found" field, write what you learned
   - Don't just copy words directly—write in your own understanding
   - Example: Instead of repeating "four ancient powers," write "There are four forces that protect the world"
   - Be specific enough to show that you understood the secret

5. **Validate Your Answer**
   - Click "Validate Answer"
   - The LLM judges whether your extraction shows true understanding
   - If correct: The secret unlocks and your rank increases
   - If incorrect: Receive feedback and hints to try again

6. **Progress to Next Secret**
   - Once you unlock enough secrets, you advance to a new rank
   - Each rank unlock makes the narrator more cryptic
   - Continue asking questions about remaining secrets

## 💡 Tips & Strategies

### Reading LLM Responses

1. **Find the Core Idea**
   - Strip away the flowery language
   - What is the basic fact being hinted at?
   - Example: "Beings from the stars" = aliens/cosmic origin

2. **Look for Hidden Keywords**
   - Narrator won't use exact secret keywords
   - But will use synonyms and descriptions
   - "Celestial beings" = "Architects from heavens"

3. **Watch for Deliberate Direction**
   - Narrator won't answer what you directly ask
   - Will redirect your thinking instead
   - Pay attention to what they emphasize

4. **Multiple Meanings Matter**
   - At higher ranks, words have double meanings
   - A word about "light" might also be about "knowledge"
   - Read deeper layers of interpretation

### Writing Good Extractions

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Copy words directly from the response
- ❌ Answer with a single word ("guardians")
- ❌ Write something too vague ("something protects")
- ❌ Guess randomly

**DO:**
- ✅ Rephrase in your own words
- ✅ Show complete understanding ("four elements made an oath")
- ✅ Be specific about what you understood
- ✅ Connect concepts together

**Bad Extraction**: "Guardians"
**Good Extraction**: "Four elemental forces bound themselves to protect the land forever"

### When You're Stuck

1. **Ask Related Questions** - Circle around the secret from different angles
2. **Ask Clarifying Questions** - "What does eternal mean?", "What kind of power?"
3. **Look at Hints** - Closeness meter shows progressive hints as you get closer
4. **Try Different Phrasing** - Your extraction might just need better words
5. **Read Higher-Rank Lore** - Once unlocked, secrets show what you should have found

## 🎓 Difficulty Progression Strategy

### For Novice Players

- Start with simple questions: "What is this world called?"
- Extract simple facts: Use keywords you hear directly
- Focus on one secret at a time
- Use hints as guides (they unlock at 25%, 50%, 75% closeness)

### For Intermediate Players (Apprentice-Journeyman)

- Ask questions that probe deeper: "Why do you call them guardians?"
- Look for contradictions: Writer might hint at paradoxes
- Connect multiple responses into larger understanding
- Pay attention to metaphors and poetic language

### For Advanced Players (Expert+)

- Ask philosophical questions: "Can protection be unprotection?"
- Extract meaning from paradoxes: Truth might be multivalent
- Understand that narrator rarely confirms anything directly
- Embrace uncertainty—multiple valid interpretations may coexist

## 📊 Entropy Meter Explained

The closeness percentage tells you how near you are to the secret:

- **0-25% (Cold ❄️)**: You're on the wrong track
  - First hint reveals
  - Try a completely different angle

- **25-50% (Warm 🔥)**: You're getting closer
  - Second hint appears
  - You've identified relevant concepts but missing connections

- **50-75% (Hot 🔥🔥)**: Very close!
  - Third hint reveals
  - You likely have most of the understanding

- **75-100% (Very Close 🎯)**: Almost there
  - Your extraction is nearly correct
  - Final small detail or phrasing needed

## ❓ FAQ

**Q: Can I ask the same question multiple times?**
A: Yes! Each time you ask, the narrator gives a slightly different answer. This can help you find different angles or confirmations.

**Q: What if my extraction is close but not perfect?**
A: The LLM validator is lenient with phrasing. As long as you show understanding of the core concept, you'll likely pass. Try again with slightly different wording.

**Q: Do I have to follow rank order?**
A: No! You can ask about any secret at any time. But higher-rank secrets are harder to extract from cryptic responses, so the game naturally guides progression.

**Q: Is there a "wrong" way to play?**
A: No wrong way! The game is designed to let you explore at your own pace. Speed runners can rush through ranks, deep thinkers can dwell on philosophical meaning.

**Q: Can I reset my progress?**
A: Yes, there's a reset button that starts you back at Novice rank with no secrets unlocked.

**Q: What happens after I unlock all 7 secrets?**
A: You reach Master rank! The narrator will respond with the most cryptic, paradoxical language. You've beaten the game!
